Projects

PMIRU - Hyperspectral polarized light camera for botanical research.

Plant and Cell Physiology 2023

Alfonso Balandra, Yuki Doll, Shogo Hirose, Tomoaki Kajiwara, Masaaki K Watahiki, et. al.
An Open Source hyperspectral polarized light camera, specifically build for botanical exploration and research. This camera lighting, polarization is automatically controlled by a software in order to capture a stack of photos, also know as reflectome. Each layer of the reflectome contains photo capture with an specific wavelengths and polarization angle. The with the same software the data can be uploaded to the cloud to be saved and visualized.

Bee Vision: An AR/HMD Vision System for Ultraviolet and Infrared Perception, Inspired by the Visual Perspective of Bees.

Not published yet

Alfonso Balandra
Bee Vision is an Augmented Reality (AR) project that transforms human vision into the perspective of a bee. Leveraging a depth stereo camera alongside a monochrome infrared/UV sensor. The software register the depth information with the infrared and UV sensor's image registration to align with the RGB camera's perspective. The initial results showcase a real-time prototype utilizing OpenCV/CUDA, achieving 20 frames per second, although optimization is still needed for Head-Mounted Display (HMD) applications.

Muni Neuron - 3d reconstruction of axial microscopic photographs of Golgi-Cox tinted neurons.

National Computational Encounter on the Clinic Computing and Medical Informatics 2009

G. Torrealba, A. Balandra, G. Flores, M. Martin
MUNI - Neuron is a software designed to construct detailed 3D models of neurons from sets of axial images captured using conventional light microscopes. Targeted towards researchers studying brain morphology, particularly those employing the Golgi-Cox method for neuron morphology analysis. This software provides an tools for visualizing and measuring neural structures.
